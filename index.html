Endangered Species Analysis: Count,
Status and Survival Rates

Species Density per State

Despite having the smallest areas, ACT and Tasmania are home to 71 and 230 threatened
species respectively, thus having a density of 3011.03 species/100k km2 and 336.35
species/100k km2. Major threats causing this are land management within urban footprint
and loss of natural temperate grassland in ACT (Van Dijk A.I.J.M. et. al., 2025), and habitat
loss and invasive species in Tasmania. (DCCEEW, 2024).

Extinct Species

EPBC Act List of Threatened Fauna shows that mammals and birds have the most extinct
species compared to other animal classes. In fact, Australia has the worst mammal
extinction rate in the world, with 10% of terrestrial mammals wiped out since European
colonisation (Australian Wildlife Conservancy, 2025).

Extinct Mammals over
the Decades

Australian mammals started going extinct in 1840 and the number continues to increase until present time. This rapid extinction is caused by widespread habitat modification and destruction, and the introduction of
invasive predators such as feral cats and red foxes (Ritchie, 2022).

Threatened Species Trend

The trend shows that birds, mammals, reptiles and frogs have higher numbers of threatened species while fish, butterflies, snails and
crayfish remain with numbers below 10. However, a recent study revealed that one to three species of invertebrates become
extinct in Australia every week (Charles Darwin University, 2024).

Threatened Status

The high number of plants in endangered
and vulnerable status highlights an urgent
conservation bottleneck, demanding immediate intervention before these populations
go into extinction.

Threatened Status by Class

The taxonomic breakdown reveals how animal classes have different threat levels. By filtering through the status, the chart demonstrates that while certain classes face localised risk, others suffer widespread displacement across the entire conservation spectrum.

Expected Survival Rate

The stacked bar chart shows the expected survival rate of species through percentage of
expected survival, potential loss and already lost. By toggling between specific groups and
indicators, it demonstrates shifts in survival probability.

Effort to Combat Extinction

Threatened Species Action Plan

Based on the Threatened Species Action Plan 2022-2032 by the Australian Government, each
action plan has specific targets that aim to be achieved. There are 22 targets in total across 8
categories (Species targets, Places and habitat targets, Threat targets, Insurance targets, First Nations targets, Planning targets, Research targets, Engagement targets).

Reserve Areas

The combined choropleth and dot map shows the density of the number of reserve areas per 100k km2 area and the location of each area. The highest density areas are in Tasmania and Victoria. In Tasmania, over 50% of its terrestrial landmass is protected due to its rugged, inaccessible terrain, strong environmental movement and unique biodiversity (Department of Natural Resources and Environment Tasmania, 2025).

Marine Protected Areas

Western Australia shows the highest total protected area, totaling up to 5.3 million hectares. One of them is the Hamelin Pool Nature Reserve which is one of the few places in the world where living marine stromatolites (biochemical rock structures built by microorganisms) can be found (Department of Biodiversity, Conservation and Attractions, 2026).
